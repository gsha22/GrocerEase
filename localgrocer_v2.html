UI Design Rationale

Design decisions and their connection to user stories

Navigation structure

The persistent top navigation and sidebar (Story 3, 12) separate the shopper experience from the owner dashboard, preventing cross-contamination of mental models. The sidebar collapses on mobile (Story 11) and is replaced by a bottom tab bar — the dominant mobile navigation pattern. The "Browse Without Account" principle (Story 12) means all navigation items are visible to unauthenticated users — login is only prompted at the moment a locked feature is needed.

The "Live" badge and freshness indicators

Stories 1 and 9 require that the platform feels alive and current, not like a static Yelp listing. The pulsing green "Updated today" badge on store cards and the de-emphasized opacity on posts older than 24h communicate freshness at a glance. Timestamps are always relative ("posted 2h ago") per Story 1's machine acceptance criteria.

Filter chips (Story 4)

Filter chips use an AND-logic multi-select with immediate visual feedback (border + background change). A "Clear filters" button appears only when at least one filter is active. Results update in-place without page reload. Labels were chosen to be culturally respectful ("Asian groceries" not "foreign food").

Reuse Past Deals (Story 9)

The past deals list shows all expired deals in reverse chronological order. A single "Duplicate →" button copies all fields into an editable form. The expiry date is always cleared on duplication — the machine criteria requires a future date, so leaving the old date would cause an immediate validation error. A success banner shows the live deal card exactly as shoppers will see it.

Post Fresh Today Updates — Owner (Story 11)

The owner posting form is minimal: item name (required) and an optional note. This mirrors the simplicity requirement from Story 11's human criteria — "post a new update in under 30 seconds." The history tab shows the owner exactly how their updates look to shoppers, including the 45% opacity de-emphasis for items older than 48 hours, so they understand the freshness system without needing documentation.

Deals auto-expiry (Story 2 + 8)

Expired deals are never shown in any shopper-facing view. On the owner side (Story 10), expired posts appear with strike-through and a note "hidden from shoppers" so owners maintain context without the data disappearing entirely — supporting the soft-delete requirement for analytics retention.

Component reuse across stories

The same store card component appears in Story 3 (directory), Story 4 (filtered results), and Story 7 (profile creation success). The deal card appears on the store profile (Story 2) and the deals directory. The filter chip pattern is reused in Story 4 and Story 7 (category selection). This shared vocabulary ensures the UI feels like one coherent application.
